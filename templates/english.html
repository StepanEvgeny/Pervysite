GLOBAL WARMING

BRIEF INFO, CAUSES OF THE PROBLEM, SOLUTIONS

BRIEF INFO!

Global warming is a long-term increase in the Earth's average temperature caused by human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas), which leads to the accumulation of greenhouse gases (such as CO2) in the atmosphere. This climate change results in the melting of glaciers, rising sea levels, altered weather patterns, and an increase in the frequency of extreme weather events such as hurricanes, droughts, and floods. As a result, ecosystems, agriculture, water supply, and human health are adversely affected. Combating global warming requires a reduction in greenhouse gas emissions and a transition to more sustainable energy sources.

REASONS FOR THE PROBLEM

  • 1. Burning fossil fuels

    Burning fossil fuels

    Fossil fuels, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, are widely used for electricity generation, heating, and transportation. When these materials are burned, they release carbon dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases. CO2 is the primary greenhouse gas that traps heat in the atmosphere and enhances the greenhouse effect. In recent decades, due to the growth of industry, transportation, and energy consumption, CO2 emissions have significantly increased, becoming one of the main factors of global warming.



  • 2. Industrial production

    Industrial production

    The industrial sector, which includes industries such as metallurgy, chemical production, and cement manufacturing, is a major source of greenhouse gas emissions. Factories and plants consume large amounts of energy, typically derived from fossil fuels, leading to CO2 emissions. Additionally, some industrial processes, such as cement production, inherently release CO2. The chemical industry is also responsible for emissions of other greenhouse gases, such as fluorinated gases, which have a very high global warming potential.

    		•
  • 3. Agriculture

    Agriculture

    Agriculture is an important source of greenhouse gases, particularly methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O). Methane is released during the digestion of livestock (cattle, sheep) and from the decomposition of organic matter in low-oxygen conditions, such as in rice paddies. Nitrous oxide is emitted from the use of nitrogen fertilizers, which are widely applied to increase crop yields. These gases have a much higher global warming potential compared to CO2, despite their significantly lower concentrations in the atmosphere.



  • 4. Deforestation

    Deforestation

    Forests play a key role in absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. However, mass deforestation, especially of tropical forests, leads to the loss of this natural CO2 absorption mechanism. Trees that are cut down or burned release the carbon dioxide stored in their biomass, which increases the overall concentration of CO2 in the atmosphere. Deforestation also results in the loss of biodiversity and disrupts the water cycle, exacerbating the effects of climate change.

    		•
  • 5. Land use, urbanization

    Land use, urbanization

    Land use changes associated with urban expansion, infrastructure development, and intensive agriculture lead to the degradation of natural ecosystems such as forests, wetlands, and pastures, which previously served as CO2 sinks. Urbanization increases the area of surfaces that heat up in the sun and release heat into the atmosphere (e.g., asphalt, concrete), which intensifies the "heat island" effect. This results in additional warming of the environment and increased energy consumption for cooling buildings.



  • 6. Transport

    Transport

    The transport sector is one of the largest sources of greenhouse gas emissions. Cars, trucks, airplanes, ships, and trains burn vast amounts of gasoline, diesel fuel, and aviation kerosene, leading to the release of CO2 and other harmful substances. Despite advancements in technology and improvements in fuel efficiency, the overall volume of emissions from transport continues to rise due to the increasing number of vehicles and the volume of freight. The contribution of aviation to global warming is particularly significant due to emissions at high altitudes, where their impact on the climate is amplified.

METHODS OF SOLUTION

  • 1. Reduce greenhouse gas emissions

    Reduce greenhouse gas emissions

    1. Switch to renewable energy: Use solar, wind, and other clean energy sources instead of coal and oil.

    2. Increase energy efficiency: Use less energy by making homes and businesses more efficient, for example, by installing energy-saving light bulbs and insulating buildings.

    3. Electrify transportation: Switch to electric vehicles and low-emission public transport.



  • 2. Use new technologies

    Use new technologies

    1. Capture and store carbon: Develop technologies that capture CO2 at factories and power plants and store it underground.

    2. Develop hydrogen energy. Use hydrogen as a clean type of fuel.

  • 3. Protect and restore nature.

    Protect and restore nature.

    1. Plant more trees: Forests absorb CO2, so restoring lost forests helps reduce carbon dioxide levels.

    2. Preserve ecosystems: Protect wetlands, mangrove forests, and other natural areas that help regulate the climate.



  • 4. Use land and agriculture wisely.

    Use land and agriculture wisely.

    1. Reduce agricultural emissions: Use fewer fertilizers and adopt more sustainable farming practices.

    2. Combine agriculture with forests: Grow trees together with agricultural crops, which helps absorb carbon.

  • 5. Transition to a sustainable economy.

    Transition to a sustainable economy.

    1. Reduce waste and recycle more: Throw away less and recycle more to reduce emissions from production and disposal.

    2. Produce and consume sustainably: Manufacture goods with minimal environmental impact and promote long-term product use.



  • 6. Collaborate at the international level.

    Collaborate at the international level.

    1. Comply with international agreements: Countries must meet emissions reduction commitments such as the Paris Agreement.

    2. Fund environmental projects: Support developing countries in their transition to clean energy and sustainable development.

  • 7. Educate and inform people

    Educate and inform people

    1. Raise awareness: Educate people about the causes and consequences of global warming and how what can be done to help.

    2. Promote an eco-friendly lifestyle: Encourage people to use public transport, reduce energy consumption and reducing meat consumption.



  • 8. Prepare for climate change

    Prepare for climate change

    1. Build sustainable infrastructure: Design buildings and roads that can withstand extreme weather conditions.

    2. Defend the coasts: Build protective structures against rising sea levels and strengthen coastlines.
